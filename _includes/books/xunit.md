Automated tests sit at the center of modern software delivery. Done well, they let you ship faster by removing whole categories of regression risk. Done badly, they become the regression risk.

Gerard Meszaros catalogs 68 patterns for writing more readable and maintainable tests, alongside a long list of common test-code smells and how to refactor your way out of them. It's a reference book more than a cover-to-cover read.

The code samples span several languages and the book is framed around the xUnit family, but the patterns transfer cleanly to NUnit, RSpec, JBehave, and any modern test framework that follows the same shape.
