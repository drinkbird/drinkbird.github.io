I joined in 2017 via **Jet.com** (Walmart's recent e-commerce acquisition at the time), as a Senior Software Engineer in the Dublin office. Within a year I was asked to lead two squads in the Supply Chain organisation, and over the following four and a half years I was promoted twice: to **Senior Software Engineer / Squad Lead** in Feb 2018, then to **Staff Software Engineer** in Jan 2019. The team continued to operate under the Jet.com brand during my early years there, before fully consolidating into Walmart Global Tech.

Across that time, my work was personally responsible for over **$20m in direct cost savings**, the reduction of production incidents and pages to a historical minimum, and the creation of three new full-blown teams.

## Selected systems delivered

- [Associate Delivery](http://bit.ly/assocdeliv)
- Delivery estimation and customer promise
- Shipment tracking
- Yard management (optimisation of inbound and outbound shipments in fulfilment centres)
- [Ship from Store: US, Mexico, Canada](http://bit.ly/shipfs)
- [Seamless returns](http://bit.ly/smreturns)
- [Shipment management with direct carrier integration and extended fulfilment capabilities](http://bit.ly/fbywmt)
- Competitive Intelligence data platform

## Responsibilities as a Staff Engineer

- Designed cloud-native systems, gathered feedback, iterated, and drove them to completion in a lean / agile / DevOps-friendly fashion
- Facilitated stakeholder, cross-team, and cross-function synchronisation: building trust, planning work, setting expectations, providing estimates, reporting progress, eliminating waste, and improving outcomes. This often meant travelling across Walmart's sites in the US and beyond.
- Worked with engineers of all levels to grow both their soft and hard skills
- Identified high-value opportunities and influenced the supply-chain technology road map
